Solution

• Buyers can verify sellers' legitimacy by checking ratings, reviews, and transaction history on reputable platforms and by using secure payment methods to avoid scams and fraudulent listings.

• Secure escrow services and reputable payment gateways can ensure financial transactions' safety, offering protection against fraud and disputes for both buyers and sellers.

• Buyers rely on detailed documentation and virtual tours, while sellers ensure comprehensive listings, facilitating informed decisions without physical inspection.

• Buyers and sellers can utilize international shipping services and digital paperwork solutions to overcome logistical challenges arising from varying locations in different regions or countries.

Persona

We identified two user personas: one who wants to exchange their car and another who prefers to buy their new car online.

Person 1 - Suresh plans to purchase a new car for the first time online.

Suresh is working as IT Manager for ford motors. He use his bike for travelling from home to office. Suresh ready to transition from public transit/bike to car ownership. With a keen eye for detail and a love for efficiency, he meticulously researches options, seeking a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle that aligns with his urban
lifestyle and adventurous spirit.

Age - 26 years old

location - Chennai

Hobby - traveling,cricket

Goals

• Performance: Seek a vehicle that aligns with their desired level of performance, whether it's fuel efficiency, horsepower, or handling capabilities.
• Safety: Prioritize safety features and crash-test ratings to protect themselves and their passengers on the road.
• Budget: Find a car that fits within their financial means, considering both the initial purchase price and ongoing costs such as insurance, fuel, and maintenance.
• Comfort and Convenience: Look for a car that provides a comfortable driving experience with features such as adjustable seating, climate control, and advanced infotainment systems.

Challenges

• Financial Constraints: Limited budget may restrict choices or force compromises on desired features
or brands.
• Research Overload: The abundance of car options, models, and features can overwhelm buyers, making it challenging to make an informed decision.
• Reliability Concerns: Fear of purchasing a lemon or a vehicle with undisclosed issues can lead to hesitation and indecision.
• Negotiating Prices: Negotiating with dealerships or private sellers can be intimidating, and buyers may struggle to secure the best deal.
• Trade-In Value: Determining the fair value of a current vehicle for trade-in purposes can be complex and lead to uncertainty during the buying process.
